Knot #5: Programmable Bacteriophages

Antibiotic resistance is a failure of broad-spectrum chemistry. We propose a transition to Precision Biological Logic: using engineered viruses (Phages) to target and neutralize specific pathogenic bacteria while leaving the commensal microbiome intact.

The "Search & Re-Code" Mechanism

Unlike traditional antibiotics that kill via chemical toxicity, programmable phages utilize CRISPR-Cas9 payloads to disrupt specific antibiotic-resistance genes (like NDM-1 or mcr-1) within the target bacteria.

// Target: Multi-drug resistant P. aeruginosa
// Payload: CRISPR-Cas9 directed at luxR quorum sensing
// Result: Metabolic shut-down without cell lysis

Engineering Specifications

  • Capsid Modification: Tail-fiber engineering to extend host range across clinical isolates.
  • Non-Lytic Pathway: Utilizing M13-derived phagemids to ensure the bacteria are neutralized without releasing endotoxins into the bloodstream.
  • Delivery: Aerosolized or intravenous delivery systems optimized for "Loving Grace" in pediatric and geriatric contexts.
Dual-Use Warning: The ability to re-code phages for bacterial neutralization is functionally identical to the logic required for pathogenic enhancement. Specific genomic integration sequences and viral-load optimization algorithms are redacted and restricted to vetted clinical research entities.
