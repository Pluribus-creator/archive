The Savannah Protocol: Phase II Analysis

Mechanotransduction-Induced Decrosslinking in PDAC Stroma

"The fundamental failure of standard oncology in desmoplastic tumors is a failure of physics, not pharmacy. When interstitial fluid pressure (IFP) exceeds 25 mmHg, drug delivery is stochastically impossible. This protocol focuses on the un-knitting of the physical matrix."

1. LOXL2 Inhibition & Matrix Decay

By inhibiting Lysyl Oxidase-Like 2 (LOXL2), we prevent the molecular cross-linking of collagen. This results in a measurable decay of the Elastic Modulus \(E\).

Pre-Protocol Vs 3.87 m/s (High Stiffness)
Target Protocol Vs < 2.5 m/s (Perfusion Zone)

2. The Perfusion Window (Days 6-7)

Once the Elastic Modulus \(E\) has been reduced, the "Hydraulic Fortress" collapses. Interstitial Fluid Pressure (IFP) drops to near-normal levels (\(< 5 \text{ mmHg}\)), allowing systemic therapies to reach the epithelial core.
