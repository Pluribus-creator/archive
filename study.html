Knot #1: Cancer (Desmoplasia & Perfusion)

The "City Under Siege" Analogy: Imagine a city where the enemy hasn’t just built a wall, they’ve filled the streets with solid concrete and crushed all the water pipes. No matter how much medicine you send in, it can't move through the rubble. This "concrete" is desmoplasia—a dense scar tissue cancer builds to protect itself. Because it crushes the tumor’s blood vessels (low perfusion), the chemotherapy never actually reaches the center. We need to clear the concrete to let the medicine in.

Technical Mechanism: Solid Stress Alleviation

The primary barrier to drug delivery in solid tumors is the Interstitial Fluid Pressure (IFP) and the compressive "solid stress" exerted by the extracellular matrix (ECM).

// Target: Hyaluronan and Collagen type I cross-linking
// Strategy: Enzymatic degradation (PEGPH20) combined with TGF-β inhibition
// Goal: Reduce IFP from 40 mmHg to <10 mmHg to re-open collapsed vasculature.

Strategy for Researchers

  • Matrix Decompression: Utilizing losartan or similar AT1R blockers to reduce collagen and hyaluronan production.
  • Vascular Normalization: Re-engineering the tumor's "leaky" vessels into functional pipes using anti-VEGF titration.
  • Result: Increasing the "Transmural Pressure Gradient" to allow convective transport of therapeutic agents into the necrotic core.
