The Savannah Protocol

Focus: Mechanotransduction-Induced Decrosslinking of PDAC Stroma

"Standard oncology fails in desmoplastic environments because the physical pressure (IFP) exceeds 25 mmHg. This is a fortress of physics. We use VDR and LOXL2 inhibition to un-knit the collagen matrix, dropping resistance to allow therapeutic perfusion."

1. LOXL2 & Matrix Stiffness

Inhibiting the LOXL2 enzyme stops the 'molecular stapling' of collagen. This causes a rapid drop in the Elastic Modulus (E) and Shear Wave Velocity (Vs).

Stiffened State (Baseline) 3.87 m/s Vs (Shear Velocity)
Primed State (Day 7) 2.10 m/s Vs (Shear Velocity)

2. Pressure Normalization

When Vs drops below 2.5 m/s, the Interstitial Fluid Pressure (IFP) collapses from 25 mmHg to under 5 mmHg. This 'opens the door' for drug delivery.
