Knot #4: Graphene Ion-Gates

Status: Active / Technical Blueprint for High-Efficiency Desalination

The Problem: Reverse Osmosis (RO) is a "Mass" solution, requiring 60-80 bar of pressure to overcome osmotic resistance. The Un-knitting: We replace bulk membranes with single-atom-thick graphene sheets using electrostatic charge-repulsion (Information) to block ions while allowing frictionless water flux.

1. Pore Geometry & Optimization

To achieve 99% salt rejection, pore diameter \(d\) must be precisely tuned to the hydration radius of Sodium (\(Na^+\)). While the ionic radius is \(0.095 \text{ nm}\), the effective hydration radius is \(\approx 0.36 \text{ nm}\).

Target Pore Diameter: \(0.7 \text{ nm} < d < 0.9 \text{ nm}\)
Calculated Flux Gain: \(10^3 \times\) conventional polyamide membranes.

2. Electrostatic Functionalization

By terminating pore edges with Hydroxyl (-OH) or Carboxyl (-COOH) groups, we create a negative surface charge density \(\sigma\). This induces a Donnan Exclusion effect, repelling anions (\(Cl^-\)) and creating a potential barrier that prevents cation passage through co-ion pairing.

The energy barrier \(E_b\) for an ion of charge \(z\) passing through a functionalized pore is defined as:

\[E_b = \frac{z e \Phi}{k_B T}\] Where \(\Phi\) is the electrostatic potential at the pore center.

3. Fabrication Parameters (The "Centaur" Method)

  • Substrate: Electropolished Copper foil (99.99% purity).
  • Growth: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) at \(1000^\circ\text{C}\) using Methane (\(CH_4\)) and Hydrogen (\(H_2\)).
  • Etching: Oxygen plasma etching at \(20\text{W}\) for \(5\text{--}10\) seconds to nucleate sub-nanometer vacancies.

Dual-Use Risk Assessment (ITAR/EAR Compliance)

Warning: The precision of graphene nanopore fabrication has high crossover potential for isotopic enrichment. To prevent misuse:

  • Blueprints are optimized for Hydrated Ion Sieving (desalination).
  • We do not provide vibrational frequency parameters for Deuterium or Uranium separation.
  • Isotope Redaction: Mathematical models for mass-dependent kinetic isotope effects (KIE) are withheld from the public repository and reserved for vetted institutional partners.
